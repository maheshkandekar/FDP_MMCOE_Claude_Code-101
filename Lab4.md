## 1. Staying current in their subject area
> "What are the latest advancements in electric vehicle battery technology in 2026?"

> "Has NVIDIA released any new GPU architecture this year? Summarize what's new."

> "What's the current state of quantum computing hardware — who are the leading players right now?"

**Teaching point:** ask the same question with search **off**, then **on** — show how the answer changes and gains dated citations.

## 2. Verifying facts before publishing/teaching something
> "Is AICTE's latest curriculum framework still the 2022 model, or has it been revised?"

> "What is the current eligibility criteria for GATE 2027?"

> "Who is the current Vice-Chancellor of [university]?"

**Teaching point:** this is the "don't trust memory for anything that changes" habit — great for notices, circulars, exam info.

## 3. Finding conferences, journals, and calls for papers
> "Find upcoming IEEE conferences on renewable energy in India in 2026, with submission deadlines."

> "Are there any current call-for-papers for embedded systems journals with a deadline in the next 3 months?"

## 4. Comparing current tools/technologies for teaching decisions
> "Compare the current versions of MATLAB Simulink vs open-source alternatives like Scilab for a control systems lab — which is better suited for a college with a limited budget?"

> "What are students and companies currently saying about which programming languages are most in-demand for embedded systems jobs in 2026?"

## 5. Job market / placement research (very relevant to placement cells)
> "What technical skills are companies currently asking for in campus placements for computer science engineers in India?"

> "What is the current average starting salary for mechanical engineering graduates from tier-2 colleges in India?"

## 6. Combining search with a document they uploaded
> "Here's our current syllabus [upload PDF]. Search the web and tell me which topics are outdated compared to current industry practice."

**Teaching point:** this pairs Lab 3 and Lab 5 skills — a strong "aha" moment for the room.

## 7. Local/practical info
> "Are there any government funding schemes currently open for student startup incubators in Maharashtra?"

> "What are the latest UGC guidelines on faculty research publication requirements?"
